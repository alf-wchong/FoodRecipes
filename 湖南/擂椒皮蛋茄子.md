# 擂椒皮蛋茄子.

## Hunan Mashed Chili with Century Egg and Eggplant

### Serves
2 to 3 as a side dish, or 1 to 2 as a main with rice.

### Ingredients
- 2 medium Japanese or Chinese eggplants
- 4 to 6 fresh green chilies or long green peppers
- 2 century eggs
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 to 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp Chinkiang vinegar or rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1/2 tsp salt, plus more to taste
- Optional: a pinch of MSG, a few cilantro leaves, a little chopped scallion

### Method
1. **Prepare the eggplant.** Cut the eggplants into long strips or chunks. Steam until fully tender, about 8 to 10 minutes. Let them cool slightly, then gently squeeze out excess water so the final dish is not watery.

2. **Char the chilies.** Dry-fry or pan-char the green chilies over medium heat until the skins blister and darken. You want a smoky, wrinkled exterior, not fully burnt. Transfer to a bowl, cover briefly, then peel off any loose skin if needed.

3. **Prep the century eggs.** Peel and cut the century eggs into wedges or rough chunks. For a more restaurant-like look, keep them slightly larger rather than smashing them completely.

4. **Make the garlic dressing.** Mash the garlic with salt, sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, and sesame oil. Add the neutral oil if you want a silkier, more aromatic finish.

5. **Assemble and mash.** In a large bowl or mortar, combine the eggplant, charred chilies, and century eggs. Use a pestle, heavy spoon, or the back of a ladle to lightly mash until the mixture is integrated but still textured.

6. **Season and finish.** Taste and adjust with more salt, soy sauce, or vinegar. Fold in cilantro or scallion if using. Serve immediately at room temperature or slightly chilled.

## Restaurant-style tips
- Use **thick, fleshy green peppers** if you want a more authentic Hunan texture and aroma.
- Don’t overmash the eggplant; leaving some fibers intact makes the dish feel more refined.
- A tiny bit of sugar matters because it balances the smoke, salt, and vinegar.
- If your chilies are very spicy, remove some seeds after charring.

## Serving style
Serve with steamed rice, congee, or as part of a Hunan meal with other cold starters. A drizzle of hot oil over the garlic right before serving can make it taste more like a restaurant version.
