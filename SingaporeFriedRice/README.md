# Singaporean Egg Fried Rice (The "Lard & Shaoxing" Edition)

This is the definitive, high-umami fried rice recipe, optimized for a high-output gas stove, a seasoned wok, and a deep respect for "leftover rice energy." This version utilizes pork lard for richness and Shaoxing wine for that authentic "perfume of the wok" flavor.

## 🚫 WARNING: The "Eh Goondu" No-No List
* No freshly cooked rice (use cold, day-old rice).
* No chili jam (Jam belongs on toast, not rice).
* No olive oil (use lard or high-smoke point oil).
* Do **NOT** rinse the rice after cooking it.

---

## 🛠 Prerequisites

* **Hardware:** Seasoned Wok, Ladle (for smashing), High-Output Gas Stove.
* **Triumvirate:** Old Rice, Fat, MSG (if you have it).

---

## 🛒 Ingredients Checklist (Based on 1 Cup Uncooked Rice)

This recipe yields a massive single portion (~3 cups cooked).

### The Foundation
* [ ] **Cold Jasmine Rice:** 3 cups (Day-old, refrigerated, broken up by hand).
* [ ] **Pork Lard (King of Fat):** 1.5 - 2 tbsp.
* [ ] **Fresh Eggs:** 2 large.

### The Aromatic Base
* [ ] **Garlic:** 3 cloves (minced FRESH. Do not use jar garlic).
* [ ] **Birds Eye Chili:** 1-2 (minced for 'Happy Tingle').
* [ ] **Spring Onions:** 2 stalks (separated: white bottoms for base, green tops for garnish).

### The Protein & Veg
* [ ] **Prawns:** 6-8 (peeled, deveined, patted dry).
* [ ] **Char Siew (Red BBQ Pork, 叉燒):** 1/2 cup (cubed).
* [ ] **Choy Sum (菜心):** 2 stalks (sliced: thin stems, wider leaves).

### The Umami Mix (Pre-mix in a small bowl)
* [ ] **Light Soy Sauce:** 1 tbsp.
* [ ] **Oyster Sauce:** 1 tsp.
* [ ] **Thai Fish Sauce:** 1/2 tsp.
* [ ] **Dark Soy Sauce:** 1/2 tsp (FOR COLOR ONLY).

### The "Aroma Booster"
* [ ] **Shaoxing Rice Wine:** 1 tbsp (The "Secret Perfume").

### The Garnish (Crunch)
* [ ] **Fried Onions & Garlic Crisps:** 1 tbsp each (Add *after* heat is off).
* [ ] **MSG (Ajinomoto):** 1/2 tsp (If you love your ancestors).

## 👨‍🍳 Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

### Phase 1: The Mise en Place (Pre-Wok)
1. **Break the Rice:** Massage the cold rice so every grain is separate. Clumps are the enemy.
2. **Pre-mix Sauce:** Mix the Light Soy, Oyster, Fish, and Dark Soy in a small bowl. This is your "Umami Bomb."
3. **Chop & Separate:** Mince garlic, chili, and white spring onions. Chop Choy Sum, separating stems and leaves. Slice the Char Siew. Thaw prawns.

### Phase 2: The Wok Action
1. **Smoking Wok:** Set your gas stove to high. When the wok smokes, add **Lard**. Swirl.
2. **Protein Sear:** Toss the **prawns** and **char siew**. Sear for 60 seconds until prawns are pink. Remove proteins and set aside.
3. **Egg Scramble:** Add a little more fat if needed. Add **eggs**. Scramble quickly, allowing them to bubble (80% cooked).
4. **Rice Dump:** Immediately dump the **rice** onto the eggs.
5. **The Ladle Smash:** Use the back of the ladle to press and toss the rice. The egg should coat the rice.
6. **Make Space:** Tilt the wok so the front side is lower and push the rice towards the back, making space for the aromatics.
7. **Aromatics:** Throw in **garlic**, **chili**, **spring onion whites**, and **Choy Sum stems**. Toss for 60 seconds until fragrant.

### Phase 3: The Umami & Aroma
1. **The Sizzle:** Pour your "Umami Mix" (sauce) around the top *edge* of the wok, not directly on the rice. TOSS vigorously to incorporate the *Wok Hei*.
2. **The "Secret Perfume":** Add **char siew**, **prawns**, and **Choy Sum leaves** back in.
3. **Shaoxing Drop:** Pour the **Shaoxing wine** in a circle around the top edge of the screaming-hot wok. It must vaporize instantly. Give the rice 3-4 massive tosses to trap that steam.
4. **Final Seasoning:** Add **MSG** now if using. TOSS.
5. **Off Heat:** Turn off the gas. Toss in **green onion tops**, **fried onions**, and **garlic crisps**.

## 📈 Optimization & Scaling

* **Want it Saltier?** Use more Light Soy or Thai Fish Sauce.
* **Want it Darker?** Use more Dark Soy.
* **Want it Crunchier?** Add more fried aromatics at the end.
